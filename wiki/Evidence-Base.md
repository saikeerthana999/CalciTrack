## 📋 Primary Guidelines

### 1. AHA/ACC 2019 Guideline on Primary Prevention of Cardiovascular Disease

**Citation:** Arnett DK, Blumenthal RS, Albert MA, et al. *2019 ACC/AHA Guideline on the Primary Prevention of Cardiovascular Disease*. Circulation. 2019;140(11):e596–e646.

**Application in CalciTrack:**
- Core ASCVD risk stratification framework (LOW / INTERMEDIATE / HIGH)
- Statin initiation thresholds and intensity recommendations
- Risk-enhancing factors framework — basis for the +5.0 enhancer scoring
- Intermediate-risk precision marker assessment (Lp(a), hs-CRP, CAC)
- Life's Essential 8 alignment

**Application in CalciTrack:**

## 🔬 Precision Biomarker Evidence

### 3. Lp(a) as a Cardiovascular Risk Enhancer

**Citation:** Wilson DP, Jacobson TA, Jones PH, et al. *Use of Lipoprotein(a) in clinical practice: A biomarker whose time has come*. Journal of Clinical Lipidology. 2022.

**Key Findings:**
- Lp(a) >50 mg/dL independently predicts ASCVD events
- Driven by genetic factors (not modifiable by diet or standard statins)
- Promotes oxidised phospholipid–mediated plaque formation and thrombosis
- Should prompt reclassification to higher risk tier in intermediate-risk patients
- Especially prevalent in South Asian populations

**Application in CalciTrack:**
- Lp(a) >50 mg/dL triggers INTERMEDIATE → HIGH (UPGRADED) reclassification
- Checkbox option for qualitative entry (+5.0)
- Numeric input for precise quantitative upgrade

---

### 4. hs-CRP and Vascular Inflammation — CANTOS Trial

**Citation:** Ridker PM, Everett BM, Thuren T, et al. *Antiinflammatory Therapy with Canakinumab for Atherosclerotic Disease*. New England Journal of Medicine. 2017;377:1119–1131.

**Key Findings:**
- Patients with hs-CRP ≥2.0 mg/L had significantly elevated cardiovascular event rates
- Anti-inflammatory therapy (canakinumab) reduced major adverse cardiac events
- Established vascular inflammation as a **causal**, not merely associative, pathway
- Validates hs-CRP ≥2.0 mg/L as a clinical threshold for escalating treatment

**Application in CalciTrack:**
- hs-CRP ≥2.0 mg/L triggers INTERMEDIATE → HIGH (UPGRADED) reclassification
- Numeric lab input for precision triage
- Evidence cited in the clinical report output

## ⚖️ South Asian Anthropometric Thresholds

### 5. WHO Asia-Pacific BMI Guidelines

**Citation:** WHO Expert Consultation. *Appropriate body-mass index for Asian populations and its implications for policy and intervention strategies*. Lancet. 2004;363(9403):157–163.

**Key Findings:**
- South/Southeast Asian populations show obesity-related comorbidities at lower BMI
- Recommended action points: overweight ≥23 kg/m², obese ≥27.5 kg/m²
- CalciTrack uses clinically pragmatic thresholds: overweight ≥23, obese ≥25

**Application in CalciTrack:**
- BMI Calculator uses South Asian–adjusted thresholds
- Normal: <23 · Overweight: 23–24.9 · Obese: ≥25

---

### 6. IDF South Asian Waist Circumference Criteria

**Citation:** International Diabetes Federation. *The IDF Consensus Worldwide Definition of the Metabolic Syndrome*. IDF. 2006.

**Key Findings:**
- South Asian–specific waist thresholds: Men >90 cm, Women >80 cm
- Significantly lower than the European criteria (Men >102, Women >88)
- Central obesity in South Asians carries higher cardiometabolic risk at smaller waist sizes

**Application in CalciTrack:**
- Waist circumference risk assessment in BMI Calculator
- Threshold-specific warnings and risk messages
