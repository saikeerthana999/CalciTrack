#  User Personas: Operationalizing Precision Cardiology

To ensure CalciTrack provides maximum clinical utility, the platform is designed around three primary user personas. This human-centered approach ensures the technical architecture meets the practical needs of the field.

---

###  1. The Community Health Worker (CHW)
**"The Frontline Screener"**

* **Objective:** To conduct rapid, high-volume cardiovascular screening in high-density urban or underserved areas.
* **Operational Context:** Uses the mobile-optimized CalciTrack interface during door-to-door outreach.
* **Key Value:** The tool’s lightweight, client-side processing allows for instant risk assessment without requiring a high-bandwidth connection or centralized database access, facilitating immediate referrals for at-risk individuals.

---

###  2. The Primary Care Resident
**"The Evidence-Based Clinician"**

* **Objective:** To provide precise, defensible risk stratification during a standard outpatient encounter.
* **Operational Context:** Utilizes the **"15-Minute Workflow"** to balance clinical depth with time constraints.
* **Key Value:** Uses CalciTrack’s ethnicity-specific multipliers to justify ordering advanced biomarkers—such as **$hs-CRP$** or **$Lp(a)$**—for intermediate-risk patients who would be overlooked by traditional, non-recalibrated ASCVD calculators.

---

###  3. The Patient
**"The Informed Decision-Maker"**

* **Objective:** To understand personal health risks and commit to long-term preventative measures.
* **Operational Context:** Reviews the visual output alongside their physician during shared decision-making.
* **Key Value:** Translates complex clinical data into a clear **Risk Trajectory**. By visualizing why **"Driving Slowly"** (early lifestyle and statin intervention) is superior to the **"Detour"** (a future emergency room crisis), the patient gains the agency needed for long-term treatment adherence.

---

| Persona | Primary Goal | CalciTrack Utility |
