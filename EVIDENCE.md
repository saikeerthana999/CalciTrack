## 1. The South Asian Risk Multiplier
Standard calculators like the PCE (Pooled Cohort Equations) often fail to account for the "South Asian Paradox." I have implemented risk enhancers based on the following:

* **AHA/ACC 2018 Guidelines:** Recognizes "South Asian Ancestry" as a key risk-enhancing factor.
* **MASALA Study:** Research from the *Mediators of Atherosclerosis in South Asians Living in America* (MASALA) study proves that South Asians have higher levels of hepatic fat and metabolic risk even at lower BMIs ($23\text{ kg/m}^2$).

## 2. Biomarkers for Precision Reclassification
The "Drive Slowly" phase of CalciTrack uses two primary biomarkers to uncover "hidden" risk.

### **Lipoprotein(a) [$Lp(a)$]**
* **The Evidence:** $Lp(a)$ is a causal, independent, and genetic risk factor for CAD. 
* **The Threshold:** My use of the $> 50\text{ mg/dL}$ threshold is supported by the **European Atherosclerosis Society (EAS)** and the **National Lipid Association (NLA)** as a trigger for more aggressive lipid-lowering therapy.

### **High-Sensitivity C-Reactive Protein [$hs-CRP$]**
* **The Evidence:** The **JUPITER Trial** demonstrated that patients with low LDL but high $hs-CRP$ significantly benefit from statin therapy.
* **The Threshold:** My use of $\ge 2.0\text{ mg/L}$ aligns with the AHA/ACC recommendations for identifying systemic inflammation as a risk enhancer.

##  3. Women's Cardiovascular Health
CalciTrack addresses the "hidden" gender gap in cardiology by integrating obstetric history into the risk engine.

* **The Evidence:** The **AHA Scientific Statement (2016)** confirms that Preeclampsia, Gestational Diabetes, and early menopause are independent predictors of long-term cardiovascular events.
* **The Threshold:** I have included these as "Hard Flags" for specialist referral, ensuring that female patients "Reach Safely" through early specialized care.
